Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2025-08-21

Bravener Consulting (“we”, “us”, “our”) respects your privacy. This Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard personal information when you visit our website, contact us, or use our services. This Policy is designed to meet the requirements of the EU/UK GDPR and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the CPRA.

This document is provided for general information and is not legal advice. Your specific obligations may vary. Consult your counsel to finalize.

1) Scope

This Policy covers personal information processed by us as a controller when you browse this website, submit forms or emails, or engage us for managed services and projects.

2) What we collect

  • Identifiers: name, business email, phone, company.
  • Device/usage data: IP address, browser type, pages viewed, and similar analytics (aggregate where possible).
  • Commercial & service data: records of services requested, proposals, and communications.
  • Sensitive data: not intentionally collected via this site; managed via contract/BAA if applicable.

3) Sources

Directly from you (forms, email, phone), automatically from your device (cookies/analytics), and from service providers supporting our operations.

4) Purposes of use

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the website and services
  • Respond to inquiries and perform contracts
  • Security, fraud prevention, and debugging
  • Comply with legal obligations
  • With consent: send updates you request

5) Legal bases (GDPR)

  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) — to provide requested services
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) — site security, service improvement, B2B communications
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) — where required (e.g., optional marketing)
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)) — recordkeeping, compliance

6) Sharing & disclosures

We share personal information with service providers (hosting, analytics, email) under contract; authorities when required by law; and parties to a business transfer. We do not sell personal information and do not share it for cross‑context behavioral advertising.

7) Data retention

We retain personal information only as long as needed for the purposes above, then delete or anonymize it per our retention practices and applicable law.

8) Your rights

GDPR (EU/UK) — access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, objection; withdraw consent anytime.

California (CCPA/CPRA) — rights to know/access, delete, correct, opt‑out of sale/share (not applicable), and limit use of sensitive personal information (not applicable). No discrimination for exercising rights.

9) Exercising rights & verification

Email Sean@bravenerconsulting.com with subject “Privacy Request”. We may verify identity; authorized agents may submit with proof.

10) Cookies & analytics

We use essential cookies and may use privacy‑respecting analytics with IP masking where feasible. Control cookies via your browser.

11) Security

We employ administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the data. No method is 100% secure.

12) International transfers

We are US‑based. Where GDPR applies and transfers occur to countries without adequacy decisions, we use safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses with vendors.

13) Children’s privacy

Not directed to children under 16. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

14) Do Not Track

We do not respond to DNT signals; use browser controls to manage cookies.

15) Contact

Controller: Bravener Consulting — 205 Fairway Dr, Lexington, NC 27292 — Sean@bravenerconsulting.com

16) Changes

We may update this Policy; the effective date reflects the latest version.

California residents: “Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information” — if our practices change, contact Sean@bravenerconsulting.com to exercise this right.
